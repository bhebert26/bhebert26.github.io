PROCESS

There's a way I like to do things.

I had been interested in programming after a robotics class I took in high school. The biggest draw to me was the problem solving aspect of it; that is, finding a solution to the problems within my code. The programs (in relation to robotics) had a specific outcome in mind, and the journey moving through iterations of said program to get to that specific outcome was fun for me.

Through my experience with coding (getting my degree in computer science and side projects), I found web development and scripting (specifically chat bots) to be what I enjoyed the most. Webdev in particular because it allows my creativity to flow, while still maintaining some aspect of problem solving.

MY MUSICAL JOURNEY

I first started guitar when I got into college around 2014 (Yeah, I know, very unique). I had an acoustic guitar that we bought from a friend, and then a used Wine Red Epiphone Les Paul Special II that we picked up from Guitar Center for $50. I didn't play much, mainly just learning those easy riffs that everyone can recognize (Smoke on the Water, Seven Nation Army, etc etc). It wasn't until about 2016 that I seriously started to try and learn things about actually playing the guitar.

After that, I had acquired a passion for music that went past listening. I got GAS (gear acquisition syndrome) and slowly increased my repitoire of instruments from an acoustic and an electric to a whole slew of things from electric guitars to basses, to pedals, and even electronic keyboards and drums.

For guitars/basses, currently I have:

  • Squire J Mascis Jazzmaster (6-string Electric Guitar)
  • Epiphone Les Paul Standard Pro (6-string Electric Guitar)
  • Squire Classic Vibe 70's Strat (6-string Electric Guitar)
  • Ibanez miKro Bass (4-string Electric Bass)
  • Michael Kelly Custom Collection Element 5 (5-string Electric Bass)
  • Ibanez AEG2011-VV (6-string Acoustic-Electric)
